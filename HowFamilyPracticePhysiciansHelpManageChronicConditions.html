What Is a Family Practice Physician?

A family practice physician is a medical doctor trained to provide primary care for people of all ages, from newborns to seniors. Unlike specialists who focus on a specific area of medicine, family physicians are generalists. They are trained in multiple areas of medicine including pediatrics, internal medicine, gynecology, geriatrics, and minor surgery.

Family physicians often build long-term relationships with their patients, getting to know their health history, lifestyle, and unique needs. This makes them ideal partners in helping prevent illness, manage chronic conditions, and provide personalized, ongoing healthcare.
